Ba Duan Jin

八段锦 — Eight Pieces of Silk. A complete health practice originating from the Song Dynasty.

八段锦

Eight Pieces of Silk

A gentle, flowing practice for health and longevity

Theory

Ba Duan Jin combines slow movements with deep breathing and mental focus to balance Yin and Yang and circulate Qi through the meridians. It works on the principle that proper energy flow creates health and vitality.

The Eight Movements

  • Hands Hold Sky — Regulates the Triple Burner (三焦)
  • Draw Bow Like Shooting Eagle — Opens the Lung meridian
  • Single Arm Lift — Balances Spleen and Stomach
  • Look Back — Relieves the Five Exhaustions
  • Shake Head and Tail — Calms excess Heart Fire
  • Grab Feet — Strengthens Kidneys and lower back
  • Clench Fist with Angry Eyes — Builds Yang energy and willpower
  • Heel Drops — Eliminates hundreds of ailments

✓ Do

  • Practice in the morning, empty stomach
  • Wear loose, comfortable clothing
  • Breathe naturally into the dantian
  • Move slowly and smoothly
  • Focus on the feeling of Qi

✗ Don't

  • Don't practice in direct sunlight
  • Don't talk while practicing
  • Don't force your breathing
  • Don't practice when sick
  • Don't over-practice (once daily is enough)

Yi Jin Jing

易筋经 — Muscle/Tendon Changing Classic. The martial foundation of Shaolin.

易筋经

Muscle/Tendon Changing Classic

Strengthen tendons and build internal power

Origin & Theory

Attributed to Bodhidharma, the founder of Shaolin martial arts, Yi Jin Jing focuses on transforming the tendons and building internal strength. Unlike external martial training, this practice develops power from within.

The 12 Exercises

  • Raise Hands to Heaven — Full body stretch, spinal elongation
  • Wei Tuo Presents Staff I — Focus and concentration
  • Wei Tuo Presents Staff II — Arm strength building
  • Pluck Stars, Change Dipper — Spinal rotation
  • Pull Nine Oxen by Tail — Arm and back strength
  • Nine Ghosts Pull Sabre — Back flexibility
  • Three Plates Fall — Leg strength and grounding
  • Green Dragon Explores Claw — Internal organ massage
  • Tiger Pounces on Food — Full body stretch
  • Bow Head, Beat Drum — Spine flexibility
  • Shake Head, Sway Tail — Hip mobility
  • Drop Tail, Raise Head — Lateral stretch

✓ Do

  • Warm up before starting
  • Hold each pose 3-5 breaths
  • Focus on tendons, not just muscles
  • Move slowly between poses
  • End with standing meditation

✗ Don't

  • Don't bounce or stretch violently
  • Don't strain beyond comfort
  • Don't skip warm-up
  • Don't hold breath
  • Don't practice on full stomach

Luohan Qigong

罗汉功 — Arhat Practice. The gentle path to inner peace.

罗汉功

Arhat Practice

Calm the mind and balance the nervous system

Philosophy

Named after the Arhats (enlightened disciples in Buddhism), Luohan Qigong is the gentlest of the three systems. It emphasizes internal feeling over external form, making it perfect for those seeking relaxation and mental clarity.

Key Principles

  • Embrace Tai Chi — Flowing arm circles
  • Push Window, Moon — Lateral extensions
  • Phoenix Dances — Wing-like arm waves
  • Follow Wind, Pull Flag — Twisting motions
  • Quiet Cultivation — Standing meditation

✓ Do

  • Move slowly and gently
  • Focus on internal feeling
  • Relax completely
  • Breathe into the dantian
  • End with stillness

✗ Don't

  • Don't force movements
  • Don't focus on appearance over feeling
  • Don't tense up
  • Don't use shallow breathing
  • Don't skip the ending stillness
