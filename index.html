Ba Duan Jin

八段锦 — Eight Pieces of Silk

Eight flowing movements that work the entire body.

Why It Works

Meridian Activation

Each movement targets specific energy channels. When Qi flows freely, organ function improves.

Gentle Stretching

Combining stretching with breath increases flexibility without strain. Stimulates connective tissues.

Mind-Body Connection

Focusing attention creates presence and reduces stress. Calms the nervous system.

1

Two Hands Hold Up the Heavens

双手托天理三焦
+

What to do: Interlace fingers with palms facing up. Push hands straight up overhead, turning palms to face the sky. Rise onto the balls of your feet. Hold for 3-5 breaths. Lower hands slowly back down.

Why it works: This full-body stretch opens the Triple Burner (Sanjiao)—the energy channel that governs how your body distributes and processes energy. The overhead reach elongates the entire spine, stretches the shoulders, and opens the front of the body. Rising onto toes engages the yang energy and completes the stretch.

Step by Step

  1. Stand with feet shoulder-width, arms at sides
  2. Interlace fingers, turn palms to face sky
  3. Push arms straight up, keeping them parallel
  4. Rise onto balls of feet
  5. Hold 3-5 slow breaths
  6. Lower feet, then lower arms

Breathing

Inhale as arms rise up. Hold briefly at the top (if comfortable). Exhale slowly as you lower. The breath should be smooth and natural—don't hold if it causes discomfort. The key is using the breath to guide energy down after the upward stretch.

Benefits

Opens Triple Burner for better energy distribution. Stretches spine and shoulders. Improves posture. Activates yang energy. Creates sense of openness in the chest.

Tips

Keep arms straight but not locked at the elbows. Don't shrug shoulders up to your ears—let them relax down. Stretch through your fingertips as if pushing into the sky.

✓ Do

  • Keep arms straight not locked
  • Stretch through fingertips
  • Relax shoulders down
  • Breathe smoothly

✗ Don't

  • Shrug shoulders
  • Hold breath
  • Bend elbows
  • Lean backward
2

Draw Bow to Shoot the Eagle

左右开弓似射雕
+

What to do: Step feet wide into a horse stance (knees bent, thighs parallel to ground). Left hand forms a "hook" shape (fist with thumb and index touching). Right hand pulls back like drawing a bowstring. Twist your torso to look at the extended left hand. Repeat on the other side.

Why it works: This movement opens the chest and lung area, improving respiratory function. The twisting motion engages the core muscles and stretches the side body. The "drawing bow" action stimulates the lung meridian, which runs through the arms. This is excellent for respiratory health and core strength.

Step by Step

  1. Step feet wide, bend knees into horse stance
  2. Left hand forms hook, extend left arm
  3. Right hand pulls back like a bowstring
  4. Twist torso to look at left hand
  5. Hold 2-3 breaths
  6. Switch sides and repeat

Breathing

Inhale as you prepare and extend. Exhale sharply as you "shoot" the bow—imagine letting the arrow fly. This creates a rhythmic pumping action that energizes the lungs. Switch sides on an inhale, then exhale as you shoot on that side.

Benefits

Opens chest and lungs. Strengthens core. Stretches side body. Improves respiratory function. Builds mental focus.

Tips

Keep your knees over your toes—don't let them go past. The "hook" hand is like holding a bow. Extend fully as if reaching something far away. Twist from the waist, not just the shoulders.

✓ Do

  • Knees over toes
  • Twist from waist
  • Full extension
  • Switch sides evenly

✗ Don't

  • Lock knees
  • Use only arms
  • Forget to switch
  • Square hips
3

Separate Heaven and Earth

调理脾胃须单举
+

What to do: Stand with feet shoulder-width. Place left palm facing up at chest level (receiving energy). Place right palm facing down at dantian level (grounding energy). Lift the left hand while pressing the right hand down. Switch sides—right hand up, left hand down.

Why it works: This movement directly stimulates the spleen and stomach meridians. In Traditional Chinese Medicine, the spleen governs transformation and transportation of nutrients. The up-down pumping motion creates movement in the digestive system, improving metabolism and energy flow.

Step by Step

  1. Stand feet shoulder-width
  2. Left palm up at chest, right palm down at dantian
  3. Inhale and lift left hand (palm up) toward sky
  4. Simultaneously press right hand down
  5. Exhale as you switch hands
  6. Repeat 5-10 times each side

Breathing

Inhale as you lift the "heaven" hand upward. Exhale as you press the "earth" hand downward. The rising hand draws energy up through the body, the pressing hand roots energy down into the earth. This creates a cycling motion of energy.

Benefits

Stimulates spleen/stomach meridians. Improves digestion. Balances energy up and down. Calms the mind. Regulates metabolism.

Tips

Keep your torso upright—don't tilt side to side. The movement should come from the shoulder, not the waist. Imagine you're holding a large ball that you're rotating between your hands.

✓ Do

  • Keep torso upright
  • Smooth transitions
  • Coordinate breath
  • Relax shoulders

✗ Don't

  • Tilt torso
  • Rush switch
  • Tense shoulders
  • Move from waist
4

Wise Owl Gazes Backwards

五劳七伤往后瞧
+

What to do: Stand with feet shoulder-width, hands resting on the fronts of your thighs. Turn your head to look behind you—first to the left, then to the right. Keep your hips facing forward throughout. Your shoulders can rotate slightly but your hips must stay stable.

Why it works: This stretches the neck and upper spine, releasing tension that accumulates from sitting and looking at screens. It stimulates the Governing Vessel (Du Mai), which runs along the spine. This movement also stretches the back muscles that are often neglected in daily life.

Step by Step

  1. Stand with feet shoulder-width
  2. Place hands on front of thighs
  3. Turn head to look over left shoulder
  4. Hold 2-3 breaths
  5. Return to center
  6. Turn head to look over right shoulder
  7. Repeat 5-10 times each side

Breathing

Inhale as you turn to one side. Exhale as you return to center or turn to the other side. Let the breath guide the rhythm—smooth and continuous. Don't hold your breath while looking back.

Benefits

Releases neck tension. Stretches upper spine. Stimulates Governing Vessel. Relieves "five exhaustions" (tiredness from sitting/standing). Improves neck mobility.

Tips

Turn your head fully—try to look behind you as far as comfortable. Keep shoulders relaxed—don't let them rise up. Your hips should face forward the entire time.

✓ Do

  • Turn head fully
  • Keep shoulders down
  • Hips stable
  • Smooth movement

✗ Don't

  • Twist only neck
  • Rotate shoulders
  • Move hips
  • Strain

Sway Head and Shake the Tail

摇头摆尾去心火
+

What to do: Squat down into a low horse stance (thighs nearly parallel to ground). Place hands on the tops of your knees. Sway your torso in a circular motion, letting your head follow the movement. Imagine a dragon swaying its body.

Why it works: This movement is said to remove "heart fire"—the excess heat and agitation that comes from stress, anger, and overthinking. The swaying motion massage the internal organs and creates gentle movement in the spine. The low position engages the lower body and grounds excess energy.

Step by Step

  1. Squat into low horse stance
  2. Place hands on tops of knees
  3. Relax neck and let head hang slightly
  4. Start swaying torso in a circle
  5. Let head follow the motion
  6. Continue for 7-10 circles
  7. Reverse direction

Breathing

Breathe naturally throughout this movement. Some traditions emphasize exhaling during the sway to release "heat." Let the breath be smooth and continuous—don't hold it. The movement should guide the breath, not the other way around.

Benefits

Removes "heart fire" (stress/agitation). Massages internal organs. Creates spine mobility. Grounds excess energy. Calms the mind.

Tips

Keep your knees bent but stable. The movement comes from your waist and hips, not just your head. Let your head bob gently—don't rigidly control it. This should feel like a gentle wave, not a violent shake.

✓ Do

  • Keep knees bent
  • Move from waist
  • Gentle circles
  • Relax upper body

✗ Don't

  • Move head alone
  • Straighten legs
  • Force circles
  • Tense up

Hold Feet to Strengthen Kidneys

双手攀足固肾腰
+

What to do: Stand tall, then bend forward from the hips (not the waist). Let your torso hang down toward your legs. Reach for your feet—grab your toes, ankles, or whatever you can reach. Breathe into your lower back. Slowly rise back up.

Why it works: This stretch targets the kidney meridian and lower back—areas that weaken with age. The forward bend compresses and releases the spine, stimulating nerve function and circulation. In TCM, the kidneys store Jing (essence), which is the foundation of vitality.

Step by Step

  1. Stand with feet shoulder-width
  2. Inhale and raise arms overhead
  3. Exhale and bend forward from hips
  4. Reach toward feet
  5. Hold and breathe into lower back
  6. Inhale and slowly rise up
  7. Repeat 3-5 times

Breathing

Inhale as you rise up to standing. Hold briefly at the top. Exhale as you bend forward. While bent, breathe deeply into your lower back—imagine breathing into the area behind your navel. This helps release tension in the lower spine.

Benefits

Strengthens kidneys (vitality). Stretches lower back. Stimulates spine. Improves flexibility. Releases hamstrings.

Tips

Bend from the hips, not the waist—imagine your hips are hinges. Keep knees slightly bent, not locked. Don't bounce or force the stretch—let gravity do the work. If you can't reach your feet, hold your ankles or shins.

✓ Do

  • Bend from hips
  • Knees slightly bent
  • Breathe into back
  • Let gravity work

✗ Don't

  • Bounce/stretch
  • Lock knees
  • Round back
  • Force reach

Clench Fists and Glare Fiercely

攥拳怒目增气力
+

What to do: Stand in horse stance (feet wide, knees bent). Clench your fists tightly. Squint your eyes slightly (the "angry" expression). Thrust one fist forward, then the other, alternating. Exhale sharply with each thrust.

Why it works: This is the most active movement, building Yang energy. The forceful exhalations activate the "fighting" reflex, which increases heart rate, blood flow, and adrenaline. The clenched fists engage the entire body's kinetic chain. This builds willpower and determination.

Step by Step

  1. Step into horse stance
  2. Clench fists tightly
  3. Make a "angry" face—squint eyes slightly
  4. Inhale, then exhale sharply while thrusting right fist forward
  5. Inhale briefly
  6. Exhale sharply while thrusting left fist forward
  7. Continue for 10-20 thrusts

Breathing

Exhale sharply with each fist thrust—this is the engine of the movement. The breath creates the power. Inhale briefly between thrusts. The exhalation should be forceful and audible—"ha!" Think of it as powering the movement with your breath.

Benefits

Builds Yang energy. Increases heart rate. Builds willpower. Engages full body. Releases tension.

Tips

Don't clench so tight that you create tension in your hands and forearms. The "angry eyes" is a slight squint, not a fierce glare. Thrust from the core, not just the arm. Keep your elbows slightly bent, not locked.

✓ Do

  • Sharp exhalations
  • Engage core
  • Thrust from body
  • Keep rhythm

✗ Don't

  • Clench too tight
  • Hold breath
  • Straighten arms
  • Use only arms

Bouncing on the Toes

背后七颠百病消
+

What to do: Rise onto the balls of your feet, lifting your heels as high as comfortable. Then drop your heels sharply back down, letting the impact create a gentle vibration through your body. Repeat 7 times.

Why it works: The gentle vibrations "shake loose" tension and stagnant energy throughout the body. This is a settling movement—after the active practice, it helps the energy integrate and settle. The impact stimulates the feet and spine, promoting circulation.

Step by Step

  1. Stand with feet shoulder-width
  2. Rise onto balls of feet (heels up)
  3. Hold briefly at top
  4. Drop heels sharply
  5. Let vibration travel through body
  6. Repeat 7 times

Breathing

Inhale as you rise onto the balls of your feet. Exhale as you drop your heels. Let the breath match the rhythm—inhale up, exhale down. This creates a meditative bouncing motion.

Benefits

Shakes loose tension. Settles energy. Stimulates feet/spine. Promotes circulation. Integrates practice.

Tips

Don't slam your heels—let them drop naturally but decisively. Keep knees slightly bent to absorb the impact. Don't hold onto anything for balance—let your body learn to balance itself. This should feel grounding, not jarring.

✓ Do

  • Rise onto balls first
  • Knees absorb impact
  • Feel vibrations
  • Stay balanced

✗ Don't

  • Slam heels hard
  • Straighten legs
  • Hold for balance
  • Jarring impact

Yi Jin Jing

易筋经 — Tendon Changing Classic

Stronger practice focused on tendons and internal power.

Why It Works

Tendon Transformation

"Yi Jin" means changing tendons—not muscles. Targets connective tissues that transmit force and store elastic energy.

Internal Power

Builds power from within. Develops ability to generate force from the core and transmit through the body.

Postural Foundation

Static poses develop structural strength. Body learns to hold itself correctly.

1

Raise Hands to Heaven

提手上式
+

What to do: Stand with feet together. Raise arms overhead, interlace fingers with palms facing up. Push straight up, stretching through fingertips. Rise onto balls of feet. Hold for 3-5 breaths. Lower slowly.

Why it works: This full-body stretch elongates the entire spine and opens all the meridians. The rising onto toes engages the posterior chain—calves, glutes, and back. This is a foundational movement that prepares the body for the rest of the practice.

Step by Step

  1. Stand with feet together, arms at sides
  2. Raise arms overhead
  3. Interlace fingers, turn palms up
  4. Push up through fingertips
  5. Rise onto balls of feet
  6. Hold 3-5 breaths
  7. Lower feet, then lower arms

Breathing

Inhale as arms rise and you stretch upward. Hold briefly at the top if comfortable. Exhale slowly as you lower. The breath should be continuous—never hold breath forcefully.

Benefits

Elongates spine. Opens meridians. Builds posterior chain. Improves posture. Full body stretch.

Tips

Keep arms straight but elbows soft—not locked. Point your fingers upward. Let your arms rotate at the shoulders. Don't shrug your shoulders.

✓ Do

  • Stretch fully
  • Arms straight
  • Point fingers

✗ Don't

  • Lock elbows
  • Shrug shoulders
  • Hold breath

Wei Tuo Presents Staff

韦陀献杵
+

What to do: Stand with feet shoulder-width. Bring palms together in front of your chest (prayer position). Press palms together firmly while maintaining the shape of your hands. Push downward slightly while keeping arms rounded.

Why it works: This develops internal connection and body awareness. The pressing engages the core while the arms maintain their shape. It builds foundational strength and teaches the body to work as an integrated unit.

Step by Step

  1. Stand feet shoulder-width
  2. Bring palms together at chest
  3. Press palms together firmly
  4. Push downward slightly
  5. Maintain arm shape
  6. Hold for 5-10 breaths

Breathing

Breathe naturally throughout. Focus on maintaining pressure, not force. The breath should remain smooth and steady. Don't hold your breath—this is about isometric tension, not movement.

Benefits

Develops internal connection. Builds core engagement. Teaches body integration. Foundation for strength.

Tips

Keep shoulders relaxed—don't let them rise. Your arms should maintain a rounded shape, like holding a ball. The pressure is inward and downward, not just pressing together.

✓ Do

  • Relax shoulders
  • Engage core
  • Maintain shape

✗ Don't

  • Strain neck
  • Push too hard
  • Tense whole body

  6. Switch sides

Breathing

Benefits

Arm strength. Back strength. Rotational power. Core engagement. Martial foundation.

Tips

Twist from the waist, not just the arms. Keep the pulling motion smooth and controlled. Don't just use arm strength—engage your back.

+

  4. Curve spine like a bow

Benefits

Back flexibility. Shoulder opening. Spine strength. Posture improvement. Lat stretch.

Tips

Keep your hips facing forward. Curve your entire spine—not just the upper back. The "bow" should be your body, curved from head to tailbone.

Luohan Qigong

罗汉功 — Arhat Practice

Gentle movements for calming the mind.

Why It Works

Calming the Mind

Gentle movements create a meditative state. Reduces stress and anxiety.

Nervous System Balance

Activates parasympathetic nervous system. Slows heart rate, reduces cortisol.

Internal Awareness

Focuses on feeling rather than form. Develops interoception.

1

Preparation

预备式
+

What to do: Stand naturally with feet shoulder-width. Arms at sides, shoulders relaxed. Close your eyes or let them rest softly downward. Breathe naturally. Center your attention on the present moment.

Why it works: This "arrival" prepares your mind and body for practice. It transitions you from the busy world into a contemplative state. Relaxing the shoulders releases tension that accumulates during the day.

Step by Step

  1. Stand with feet shoulder-width
  2. Arms at sides
  3. Relax shoulders
  4. Close eyes or soften gaze
  5. Breathe naturally
  6. Center attention
  7. Hold 1-2 minutes

Breathing

Breathe naturally into your dantian (lower abdomen). Don't control the breath—just observe it. Let it find its own rhythm. The breath should be quiet and effortless.

Benefits

Centers attention. Releases shoulder tension. Transitions to practice. Present moment awareness.

Tips

Don't force anything—this is about arriving, not achieving. Let your joints relax. Feel your feet on the ground. Don't rush this step.

2

Embrace Tai Chi

怀抱太极
+

What to do: Hold your arms in front of your body as if holding a large ball. Palms face each other, about shoulder-width apart. Move your hands in circles—clockwise, then counter-clockwise.

Why it works: This creates a sense of center and grounding. The circular motion balances energy flow. It develops body awareness and coordination. The "ball" helps you feel your body's relationship to space.

Step by Step

  1. Hold arms in front
  2. Palms face each other
  3. Imagine holding a large ball
  4. Move hands in circles
  5. Clockwise 7 times
  6. Counter-clockwise 7 times

Breathing

Inhale as hands move in one direction, exhale as they move in the other. Or simply breathe naturally and let the breath guide the rhythm naturally.

Benefits

Center and grounding. Body awareness. Balance energy. Coordination. Internal focus.

Tips

Feel the "ball" in your hands—even though it's imaginary. The circles should be smooth and continuous. Keep shoulders relaxed.

Push Window, Moon

推窗望月
+

What to do: Extend right arm to the right side at shoulder height, palm facing forward. Place left hand at your chest. Turn your head to look at the extended hand. Push slightly as if pushing open a window. Switch sides.

Why it works: This lateral extension opens the side body and ribcage. It stretches the intercostal muscles between the ribs. The turning head stimulates the neck and upper spine.

Step by Step

  1. Extend right arm to right
  2. Left hand at chest
  3. Turn head to look at right hand
  4. Push slightly
  5. Hold 2-3 breaths
  6. Switch sides

Breathing

Inhale as you prepare and extend. Exhale as you push. Let the breath power the gentle push.

Benefits

Lateral stretch. Ribcage opening. Neck release. Side body awareness.

Tips

The push is gentle—more of an intention than a forceful action. Keep your hips facing forward. Feel the stretch along your side body.

Phoenix Dances

凤凰起舞
+

What to do: Let your arms wave gently like wings. Move from side to side, swaying your upper body. The motion should be flowing and graceful—like a phoenix bird dancing.

Why it works: This releases tension in the shoulders and upper back. The gentle swaying creates a relaxation response in the nervous system. It's excellent for stress relief and emotional release.

Step by Step

  1. Let arms hang loosely
  2. Begin swaying side to side
  3. Let arms wave like wings
  4. Flowing motion
  5. Continue for 30-60 seconds

Breathing

Match breath to sway—inhale one direction, exhale the other. Or breathe naturally and let the movement guide the breath. The key is smoothness.

Benefits

Shoulder release. Stress relief. Nervous system calm. Emotional release. Upper body mobility.

Tips

Let your arms be heavy—not lifted forcefully. The movement comes from your body, not your will. Imagine you're floating.

5

Follow Wind, Pull Flag

顺风扯旗
+

What to do: Extend one arm up at an angle, the other down. Twist your torso toward the raised arm—like pulling a flag or rope. Switch sides. The motion should be smooth and flowing.

Why it works: This gentle spinal twist massage the internal organs. It stretches the obliques and back muscles. The diagonal reach creates length through the spine.

Step by Step

  1. Right arm up at angle
  2. Left arm down
  3. Twist toward right
  4. Like pulling a flag
  5. Hold briefly
  6. Switch sides

Breathing

Exhale as you twist in each direction. Let the twist happen naturally—don't force it.

Benefits

Spinal twist. Organ massage. Oblique stretch. Diagonal length.

Tips

The movement is gentle—not a deep twist. Keep both feet grounded. Let the twist happen in your spine, not your hips.

6

Quiet Cultivation

静养
+

What to do: Stand still. Place hands on your dantian (lower abdomen)—left hand over right for women, right over left for men. Breathe gently. Meditate silently.

Why it works: This integration of practice allows the benefits to settle. It calms the mind and allows energy to circulate. Standing meditation consolidates the practice.

Step by Step

  1. Stand still
  2. Place hands on dantian
  3. Close eyes if comfortable
  4. Breathe gently
  5. Meditate silently
  6. Hold 2-5 minutes

Breathing

Deep, slow breathing into the dantian. The breath should be quiet and effortless. Don't control it—just let it flow naturally.

Benefits

Integration of practice. Mind calming. Energy settling. Deep relaxation. Practice consolidation.

Tips

Don't rush this—it's the most important part. Let go of any thoughts. Simply be present.
