Ba Duan Jin

八段锦 — Eight flowing movements that work the entire body.

Why It Works

Meridian Activation

Each movement targets specific energy channels. When Qi flows freely, organ function improves.

Gentle Stretching

Stretching combined with breath increases flexibility without strain.

Mind-Body Connection

Focusing attention creates presence and reduces stress.

1

Two Hands Hold Up the Heavens

双手托天理三焦
+

What: Interlace fingers, palms up. Push overhead, turn palms to sky. Rise onto toes. Hold 3-5 breaths. Lower.

Why: Full-body stretch opens the Triple Burner (energy distribution). Elongates spine, stretches front body.

Breathing

Inhale as arms rise. Hold at top. Exhale lowering.

✓ Do

  • Arms straight not locked
  • Stretch through fingers
  • Relax shoulders

✗ Don't

  • Shrug shoulders
  • Hold breath
  • Lean back
2

Draw Bow to Shoot the Eagle

左右开弓似射雕
+

What: Horse stance. Left hand hook, right hand pulls bowstring. Twist torso. Switch sides.

Why: Opens chest, engages core, stretches side body. Lung meridian runs through arms.

Breathing

Inhale prepare, exhale "shoot".

✓ Do

  • Knees over toes
  • Twist from waist
  • Full extension

✗ Don't

  • Lock knees
  • Use only arms
  • Skip sides
3

Separate Heaven and Earth

调理脾胃须单举
+

What: Feet shoulder-width. Left palm up, right palm down. Lift left, press right. Switch.

Why: Stimulates spleen/stomach meridians. Up-down motion pumps energy through digestion.

Breathing

Inhale lift, exhale press.

✓ Do

  • Torso upright
  • Smooth transitions
  • Coordinate breath

✗ Don't

  • Tilt torso
  • Rush
  • Tense shoulders
4

Wise Owl Gazes Backwards

五劳七伤往后瞧
+

What: Hands on thighs. Turn head to look behind. Hips stay forward. Alternate.

Why: Stretches neck/upper spine. Releases tension from sitting. Stimulates Governing Vessel.

Breathing

Inhale one side, exhale other.

✓ Do

  • Relax shoulders
  • Turn fully
  • Hips stable

✗ Don't

  • Twist only neck
  • Move shoulders
  • Look down
5

Sway Head and Shake the Tail

摇头摆尾去心火
+

What: Low horse stance. Hands on knees. Sway torso in circles, head follows.

Why: Removes "heart fire" (stress, agitation). Massages organs, creates spine movement.

Breathing

Natural breathing.

✓ Do

  • Knees bent
  • Move from waist
  • Circle smoothly

✗ Don't

  • Head alone
  • Straighten legs
  • Rush
6

Hold Feet to Strengthen Kidneys

双手攀足固肾腰
+

What: Bend from hips (not waist). Reach toward feet. Hold toes/ankles. Breathe into lower back.

Why: Targets kidney meridian and lower back. Compresses/releases spine. Kidneys store essence (Jing).

Breathing

Inhale rise, hold, exhale bend.

✓ Do

  • Bend from hips
  • Knees slightly bent
  • Feel stretch

✗ Don't

  • Bounce/force
  • Lock knees
  • Round back
7

Clench Fists and Glare Fiercely

攥拳怒目增气力
+

What: Horse stance. Clench fists. Squint eyes slightly. Thrust fists forward alternately with sharp exhales.

Why: Most active—builds Yang energy. Sharp exhalations increase heart rate. Builds willpower.

Breathing

Exhale sharply each thrust. Inhale between.

✓ Do

  • Sharp exhalations
  • Engage core
  • Focus eyes

✗ Don't

  • Clench too tight
  • Hold breath
  • Straighten arms
8

Bouncing on the Toes

背后七颠百病消
+

What: Rise onto balls of feet, drop heels sharply. Repeat 7 times. Feel vibration through body.

Why: Vibrations shake loose tension. Settling movement—helps integrate practice.

Breathing

Inhale rise, exhale drop.

✓ Do

  • Rise balls first
  • Knees absorb
  • Feel vibration

✗ Don't

  • Slam heels
  • Straighten legs
  • Hold for balance

Yi Jin Jing

易筋经 — Tendon Changing Classic. Stronger practice for tendons and internal power.

Why It Works

Tendon Transformation

"Yi Jin" means changing tendons—not muscles. Targets connective tissues that store elastic energy.

Internal Power

Builds power from within. Generates force from the core, transmits through body.

Postural Foundation

Static poses develop structural strength. Body learns to hold itself correctly.

1

Raise Hands to Heaven

提手上式
+

What: Feet together. Arms overhead, interlace fingers. Stretch up, rise onto toes. Hold 3-5 breaths.

Why: Full-body stretch. Elongates spine, opens all meridians.

Breathing

Inhale rise, hold, exhale lower.

✓ Do

  • Stretch fully
  • Arms straight
  • Point fingers

✗ Don't

  • Lock elbows
  • Shrug
  • Hold breath
2

Wei Tuo Presents Staff

韦陀献杵
+

What: Feet shoulder-width. Press palms together at heart. Press down gently while maintaining arm shape.

Why: Develops internal connection. Core engages while arms hold structure. Builds foundation.

Breathing

Natural breathing.

✓ Do

  • Relax shoulders
  • Engage core gently
  • Maintain shape

✗ Don't

  • Strain neck
  • Push too hard
  • Tense body
3

Pluck Stars, Change Dipper

摘星换斗
+

What: Right hand forms hook down, left hand raises overhead. Look at raised hand. Switch.

Why: Spinal rotation with hip stability. Opens shoulder, stretches side body.

Breathing

Inhale raise, hold, exhale switch.

✓ Do

  • Hips stable
  • Turn fully
  • Extend through fingers

✗ Don't

  • Lean sideways
  • Twist only neck
  • Rush
4

Pull Nine Oxen by Tail

倒拽九牛尾
+

What: Horse stance. Right arm forward, left back. Pull in ox tail motion. Switch.

Why: Builds arm/back strength. Pulling engages lats and core. Develops rotational power.

Breathing

Exhale pull, inhale return.

✓ Do

  • Twist from waist
  • Use back muscles
  • Full extension

✗ Don't

  • Arms only
  • Square hips
  • Small movements
5

Nine Ghosts Pull Sabre

九鬼拔马刀
+

What: Right hand behind back, left over head. Curve spine like a bow. Look at hand. Switch.

Why: Extreme back flexibility. Opens shoulder, stretches spine. Strengthens back muscles.

Breathing

Inhale arch, exhale hold.

✓ Do

  • Curve like bow
  • Hips forward
  • Feel stretch

✗ Don't

  • Flatten back
  • Rotate hips
  • Force
6

Three Plates Fall to Ground

三盘落地
+

What: Feet wide. Hands in front, palms down. Squat, press hands down. Rise, hands up. Repeat.

Why: Leg strength and grounding. The pressing down activates the lower body.

Breathing

Exhale down, inhale up.

✓ Do

  • Knees over toes
  • Press down firmly
  • Full range

✗ Don't

  • Knees past toes
  • Go too low
  • Rush
7

Green Dragon Explores Claw

青龙探爪
+

What: Horse stance. Left hand claw at chest. Right hand pushes through. Twist. Switch.

Why: Internal organ massage. Twisting motion stimulates digestion and organ function.

Breathing

Exhale push, inhale return.

✓ Do

  • Twist from waist
  • Full extension
  • Focus internally

✗ Don't

  • Use only arm
  • Keep hips square
  • Surface only
8

Tiger Pounces on Food

卧虎扑食
+

What: Lunge forward. Hands on ground/bed. Dip chest forward. Switch legs.

Why: Full body stretch. Reaches deep muscles. Opens hip flexors.

Breathing

Inhale lunge, exhale hold.

✓ Do

  • Full extension

✗ Don't

  • Strain lower back
  • Knees past toes
  • Hold too long
9

Drop Tail, Raise Head

Luohan Qigong

罗汉功 — Arhat Practice. Gentle movements for calming the mind.

Why It Works

Calming the Mind

Gentle movements create a meditative state. Reduces stress and anxiety.

Nervous System Balance

Activates parasympathetic nervous system. Slows heart rate, reduces cortisol.

Internal Awareness

Focuses on feeling rather than form. Develops interoception.

1

Preparation

预备式
+

What: Stand naturally. Arms at sides. Relax shoulders. Breathe naturally. Center attention.

Why: Arrives in the present moment. Releases tension before practice begins.

Breathing

Natural breathing into dantian.

2

Embrace Tai Chi

怀抱太极
+

What: Arms in front, palms facing. Move in circles, like holding a big ball. Clockwise then counter.

Why: Balances energy. Creates sense of center and grounding.

Breathing

Inhale one direction, exhale other.

3

Push Window, Moon

推窗望月
+

What: Right hand pushes right, left hand at chest. Turn head to look. Switch sides.

Why: Lateral extension. Opens side body, stimulates rib cage.

Breathing

Inhale prepare, exhale push.

4

Phoenix Dances

凤凰起舞
+

What: Arms wave like wings. Side to side sway. Flowing, gentle motion.

Why: Releases tension in shoulders and back. Creates relaxation response.

Breathing

Match breath to sway rhythm.

5

Follow Wind, Pull Flag

顺风扯旗
+

What: One arm up, one down. Twist torso. Like pulling a rope.

Why: Spinal twist with gentle force. Massages internal organs.

Breathing

Exhale twist.

6

Quiet Cultivation

静养
+

What: Stand still. Hands on dantian. Breathe gently. Meditate.

Why: Integration of practice. Allows benefits to settle. Calms the mind.

Breathing

Deep, slow, into dantian.
